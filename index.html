TIPS ON HOW TO PREVENT FIRE







Avoid Octopus Wiring

Avoid plugging-in multiple products in one wall socket. Octopus Connection causes fire when the total current that passes through the wires exceeds the indicated rating, this can cause heat inside the wiring and ultimately cause fire.Unplug appliances when not in use. Use high quality and high standard electrical products when replacing wires or other components. Do not use damaged appliances.

Don't Smoke at Home

Tobacco is manufactured to burn at over 700 degrees C and to stay alight, meaning it can remain smouldering and start a fire. When smokers fall asleep with a lit cigarette in hand, clothes, bedding or furniture can easily catch alight. Also, harmful chemicals creep from room to room, waiting for your lungs to breathe them in. Second-hand smoke contains more than 4,000 toxic chemicals. The particles are smaller than dust and drift easily as you move through the house and open doors. JUST DON'T SMOKE! DO YOU WANT YOUR HOUSE TO BURN OR HAVE LUNG CANCER?

Never Leave Candles Unattended




Unattended candles start many preventable fires that sadly lead to tragic fire deaths. Often, the fire is sparked by things that easily catch on fire. This includes curtains or paper being left too close to the flame.

Invest on a Fire Extinguisher




Fire extinguishers can be a small but important part of the home fire safety plan. They can save lives and property by putting out a small fire or suppressing it until the fire department arrives.
